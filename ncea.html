All About NCEA | What Is NCEA ? !

The National Certificate of Educational Achievement ( NCEA ) is main national qualification for secondary school students in New Zealand. NCEA is recognised by employers and used for selection by universities and polytechnics, both in New Zealand as well as overseas. Between the School years of Year - to Year 13 ( five years of secondary school) is when students will choose and study a number of different subjects which may include academic subjects such as English, Mathematics /Science/ Social Sciences areas of academic learning ranging from the The Arts to Technology . Each secondary school student gains credits from NCEA achievement standards under NCEA Levels 1 , 2 or 3. The higher the NCEA Level the more advanced the learning material becomes as well as difficult to gain credits to pass your year and end of year exams . Each NCEA Level has its own requirement of how many credits are needed in order to move on to the next Level. These are 80 Credits at NCEA Level 1 and 60 Credits for Level 2 and 3.

These credits are gained through the achievement standards of your subject under Internals and Externals. Where Internals are marked by the management of your school importantly the subject factually or department the Internal belongs to, Externals are End of your Exams are created by the Ministry Of Education under the NZQA and are marked within them as well. Each Internal and External is worth a specific number of credits which when gained go towards your NCEA level requirement of credits. In order to gain a grade of Merit or Excellence in your internals and externals in order to gain NCEA credits, focus on your subject study and the efficiency of your study is required in order to pass exams and internal assessments throughout your school year.
